PhD Literature Review Writing Help

Developing a critical literature review for your PhD thesis is critical to establishing a correct research gap and thereby selecting correct research objectives. PhD Guidance provides complete assistance from searching for relevant sources/citations for literature review and also writing the chapter. Presented below are some steps on how to develop a literature review

Find A Topic

When you need to review literature for your thesis, it is important to find a topic first. This can be done while searching the existing literature. When you know the amount and relevance of your searched literature, you may tweak your topic by modifying or narrowing it down.

Locate And Review Literature

In this step, you should do a generic reading of your searched literature. This helps you in understanding more about the already covered topics, but also about those that have the scope of further investigation. You may also go through the linked theories and models that can be the basis of detailed research.

Establish The Writing Purpose

Identify the aim of writing a literature review. Most of the times, it is about tracing ethical, legal and political issues and other historical developments. You may also need this for judging the effectiveness of treatment or identifying the existing gaps in research.

Evaluate Literature

To give due importance to critical and relevant results in your study, it is also essential to interpret and evaluate literature. When you discuss about your study topic, it is important to develop your discussion based on literature that is built on relevant theories.

Do Literature Synthesis

Based on your study‚Äôs design, framework and research model, it is required to synthesize your literature or group it. If there are any noticeable cracks, then those can be used to explore the research gap.

Develop The First Draft

When you start writing the initial draft of your literature review, make sure you form meaningful headings and subheadings. Use your preferred or recommended professional citation style in a consistent manner. All relevant theories should also be developed properly.

Peer Review The Draft

After you prepare the first draft, get it peer-reviewed to remove plagiarism and unnoticed errors. Based on the peer review feedback, make appropriate changes to shape your work well.

PhD Research Methodology Chapter Help

Research methodology is the basis of your study. You should have an intensive knowledge of all aspects of research, while you are writing it. You need to execute your ideas with precision to develop it well. While many research students think about their data collection tools when they start writing about the methodology, it is also important to look at some other aspects according to Saunders.

Some important points to be noted in this concept include the following

The type of reality you have been investigating triggers some beliefs. The set of such beliefs is termed as the research philosophy. Its assumptions actually justify your methodology.

Research philosophy can be categorized in three ways ontology, epistemology and axiology on the basis of your understanding. Each of the thought processes differs in the approach you adopt toward the understanding of your methodology.

The philosophy of positivism assumes that reality is independent of subjects, the philosophy of constructivism assumes that observations are interpreted based on subjects‚Äô response differences.

The deductive approach of research is regarding the deduction of hypotheses from theories, making them operational, and testing them. On the other hand, the inductive approach is regarding the building up of theories based on people‚Äôs ways of interpreting certain events.

Based on your research topic and its intrinsic qualities, the choice between primary and secondary data or between qualitative and quantitative research is made. When there are specific queries and their contexts, answers are sought through a mixed kind of methodology.

The Research methodology chapter will cover Developing a research design, it is done once the scholar is aware of the research gap, Developing a questionnaire, it‚Äôs done when objectives are made, Reliability test or Pilot Study is done by collecting some samples(30-40), Data collection is done on the basis of questionnaire developed, Data analysis is a descriptive and factor analysis or SEM.

Qualitative Analysis Help For PhD Research

When you perform qualitative research, you mostly study the context of a problem and the involved issues in order to find answers to your research problem. Qualitative research generally uses techniques, such as observation and personal interviews, to answer questions. It requires you to study the behavior of participants for assessing the causes of certain events and actions displayed by them. Qualitative approach varies significantly from quantitative in the sense that it investigates the ‚Äòhow and why‚Äô of things.

Why Choose a Qualitative Study?

Through a qualitative study, you can understand an unknown phenomenon and find out its causes. Several causal explanations in ethnographic research are now provided through such qualitative studies. This approach is adopted by a range of studies performed in the field of social sciences, including grounded theory studies. Ranging from content analysis and the study of journals to taking field notes, there are several methods of data collection used by qualitative studies.

Systematic Literature Review

Systematic Literature Review (SLR) can be seen as a step ahead taken in literature review. It also searches, identifies, selects and then analyses the existing literature to answer the questions raised in the current research work. However, the critical approach is enhanced in a systematic approach to fulfill the purpose of providing better credibility and clarity. This rigorous process does not just involve finding the accurate literature for the study but also strategically planning the methods of collecting those studies. The criteria and approach of your study also remain the focal point while conducting the SLR, as it is done with the purpose to improve the existing knowledge about the discoveries in the topic area, to evaluate the research methodologies that have been used earlier and to come up with better theories and analysis.

Conceptual Framework Design

Conceptual Framework or Research Framework can be defined as the illustrations which are used to highlight the important variables which show the findings from the research. Most of the time, these frameworks are based on the literature review of the research. It most importantly shows the cause and effect relationship between the dependent and independent variables. This simple structure can become trickier with a large number of variables affecting the data and when there are other variables like moderating variables, mediating variables and controlling variables. Therefore, it becomes important to draw a conceptual framework in an illustrated graphic for a better clarity.
